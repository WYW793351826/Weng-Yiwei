About Me

Who Am I?

Hi I'm Yiwei. My research interest lies in 3D concrete printing, including system development, material optimizaiton, and control integration. In my Ph.D. period, we developped a robotic-based and gantry-based system for 3D concrete printing. A type of novel printable fiber reinforced cementitious materials was designed for large-scale printing application. In addition, an automatic concrete delivery system was designed and fabricated.

Now, I am working on devlopping printable strain hardening cementitious matrials for large-scale printing, and upgrading our current delivery system to integrate it with the concrete printer. Additionally, I am also working on integrate Building Information modeling wiht 3D concrete printing.

Projects

Projects

Development and optimize 3D printable cementitious material for large-scale printing application 2015-present

In this project, various type of printable cementitious materials are developped through material rheological and mechanical performance, and the large-scale printing is conducted to test the printability of developped material.

3D concrete printing system development 2015-present

Two types of concrete printer are designed and fabricated, e.g. gantry-based and robotic-based concrete printer.

Integrated Building Information Modeling with 3D concrete printing 2015-present

In this project, a novel script package is proposed and integrate BIM and 3D cocnrete printer to achiever a one-click printing process.

Surface patterning atop poly (methyl methacrylate) 2014-2015

In this project, three types of microlens arrays were fabricated. One was fabricated with the same size atop a PMMA plate from same sized indents. As tested, these microlenses have the same focus length. The second sample was manufactured the different sized microlens arrays atop the same kind of PMMA plate. In the third sample, microlens arrays were produced atop a curved PMMA sample. Surface scanning experiment and projection experiment were conducted to test the optical properties of these microlens arrays.
